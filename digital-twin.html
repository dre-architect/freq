The Problem

The Manual Process Today

Every day, thousands of barges on America's inland waterways undergo a manual cargo drafting process that hasn't changed in decades. Here's what that looks like in practice.

4+ Hours Per Survey

A crew physically walks onto the barge, reads draft marks painted on the hull with their eyes, and radios numbers to shore one at a time. Six measurement points, multiple readings each, weather delays, radio misreads, and manual calculation — every barge, every load.

Crew on Deck Means Risk

Every person walking a barge deck during cargo operations is exposed to fall hazards, moving equipment, and unpredictable weather. Man-overboard incidents are a real and documented danger in inland waterway operations — the leading non-navigational fatality in the industry.

1–3% Measurement Error

Human visual readings from hull marks are inherently imprecise. Parallax error, wave action, reading conditions, and transcription mistakes compound at every step. A 1% error on a 2,000-ton barge is 20 tons of cargo uncertainty — real revenue lost or compliance exposure on every load.

The FREQ AI Solution: 15 Minutes. Zero Crew. Sub-Inch.

Ghost LiDAR scans the hull autonomously across all six measurement stations. The Sophisticated Operational Lattice monitors stability and displacement in real-time. A digital draft survey generates automatically, ready for immediate submission. Watch the complete operation in the simulation below.
